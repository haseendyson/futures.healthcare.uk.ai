💡 Why this matters?

Healthcare organisations need better ways to understand what communities value, trust, and want from future care. Existing engagement methods often miss the reflective, nuanced, and culturally grounded perspectives that shape real-world healthcare decisions.

⚙️ What this project does?

We are developing a conversational micro-narrative method that uses AI to support reflective storytelling, generate narrative summaries, and allow participant review—helping preserve voice, ownership, and depth while improving scalability.

🚀 Why it is promising?

The approach combines qualitative richness with a more structured, repeatable workflow, creating potential value for inclusive service design, public engagement, health research, and innovation across diverse communities.

The approach

From lived experience to usable strategic insight

The method combines structured conversational prompting, narrative synthesis, and participant review. Rather than relying only on fixed-response instruments or resource-heavy interviews, it creates a more scalable way to gather reflective, story-based insight without losing participant perspective.

The result is a workflow that can support richer qualitative engagement while remaining practical for research, design, and innovation contexts where trust, nuance, and cultural responsiveness matter.

Impact potential

Built for relevance beyond a single study

This work begins with South Asian communities in the UK, where questions of trust, access, prevention, family, and cultural context are especially important. But the broader opportunity is methodological: a reusable model for community-informed insight generation that can support healthcare strategy, participatory design, and future service innovation at greater scale.
